Dobutamine is primarily an inotrope that acts on Beta-1 receptors to increase contractility of the heart. It's effect on Beta-2 also decreases systemic vascular resistance (SVR) resulting in vasodilation. In some cases, together these will improve cardiac output by decreasing afterload. Increased force of contraction coupled with less resistance will make it easier for the heart to eject blood out with each contraction. The direction stimulation of beta receptors on the myocardium effects a swift increase in force of contraction.

Rationale: Increasing contractility will not improve cardiac output if there is no fluid volume in the vessels. The decrease in systemic vascular resistance (SVR) from beta 2 skeletal muscle vasodilation will decrease venous return and preload; this will further reduce cardiac output.
